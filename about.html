Elifin Öküzü is

an open source etymological dictionary prepared by Python-Django class at Mustafa Akgül Free Software Summer Camp.

The project bases on mapping the words at Sevan Nişanyan's "Elif's Ok or The Surprises Book".

There are more than 1000 explanations of what some words meant and how they sounded 600 or 2,000 years ago at Nişanyan's book. It is full of surprises. The words we use has an unbeliveable history. The history of Turkish language is about 1300 years but the words we use today has a history of thousands years. We run across some of the words at Homeros's sources or some others at Zarathustra religion. Mustafa Akgül

Mustafa Akgül was a Turkish computer scientist who was the key figure of the acceptance of the Internet in Turkey. He went to the University of Waterloo, Canada and got his PhD in Combinatorics and Optimisation. He started with the motto of "Internet is life". At this conference; people come together and started a community named Turkey Linux Users Community and made their first speech about free software again at that organization.It was Mustafa Akgül's dream to organize a Linux Summer Camp and he made it real at 2010.
